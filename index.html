Hz to Rad/s

Introduction

Hertz (Hz) and radians per second (Rad/s) are both units of frequency. The Hertz is the unit of frequency in the International System of Units (SI) and is defined as one cycle per second. It is an important unit in the study of waves and oscillations.

Radians per second, on the other hand, are a unit of angular frequency or angular velocity. In many areas of physics and engineering, angles are often measured in radians rather than degrees. One radian per second is equal to one radian (approximately 57.3 degrees) moving along the circumference of a circle per second.

Conversion from Hz to Rad/s

To convert frequency from Hertz to radians per second, you need to use the formula:

Rad/s = 2πf

where f is the frequency in Hertz and π is a mathematical constant whose value is approximately 3.14159.

Therefore, to convert 1 Hz to Rad/s, you can multiply 1 by 2π to get approximately 6.28318 Rad/s.
